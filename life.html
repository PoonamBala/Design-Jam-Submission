PERSONAL LIFE

  • EARLY LIFE

    Born : September 25, 1968, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania (52yrs present)
    Parents: Caroline (née Bright), a Philadelphia school board administrator, and Willard Carroll Smith Sr., a U.S. Air Force veteran and refrigeration engineer
    Siblings: He has an elder sister named Pamela and two younger siblings, twins Harry and Ellen
    He grew up in West Philadelphia's Wynnefield neighborhood, and was raised Baptist

    Smith attended Overbrook High School. While it has been widely reported that Smith turned down a scholarship to attend the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), he never applied to college because he "wanted to rap." According to Smith, "My mother, who worked for the School Board of Philadelphia, had a friend who was the admissions officer at MIT. But I had no intention of going to college."

  • PERSONAL LOVE LIFE

    1992

    Smith married Sheree Zampino in 1992. Their son Willard Carroll "Trey" Smith III was born on November 11, 1992.Trey appeared in his father's music video for the 1998 single "Just the Two of Us". He also acted in two episodes of the sitcom All of Us, and has appeared on The Oprah Winfrey Show and the David Blaine: Real or Magic TV special.

  • 1997

    Smith married actress Jada Koren Pinkett in 1997. The pair produce films through their joint production company, Overbrook Entertainment and Westbrook Inc. Together they have two children: Jaden and Willow Smith.

    FAMILY

    Together they have two children: Jaden Christopher Syre Smith (born 1998), his co-star in The Pursuit of Happyness and After Earth; and Willow Camille Reign Smith (born 2000), who appeared as his daughter in I Am Legend.

  • Philanthrophy

    Smith gave $1.3 million to charities in 2007, of which $450,000 went to two Christian ministries, and $122,500 went to three Scientology organizations; the remaining beneficiaries included "a Los Angeles mosque, other Christian-based schools and churches, and ... the Yitzhak Rabin Memorial Center in Israel."[101] Smith and his wife have also founded a private elementary school in Calabasas, California, the New Village Leadership Academy.[102] Federal tax filing showed that Smith donated $1.2 million to the school in 2010.

  • Political

    1992

    Smith donated $4,600 to the 2008 presidential campaign of Democrat Barack Obama.[104] On December 11, 2009, Smith and his wife hosted the Nobel Peace Prize Concert in Oslo, Norway to celebrate Obama's winning of the prize.

    2012

    In 2012, Smith stated he supported legalizing same-sex marriage.

    2021

    In 2021, Smith announced that production of his upcoming film, Emancipation, is being pulled from Georgia because of the recent passage of the Election Integrity Act of 2021, which critics view as a restrictive voting law. Smith and director Antoine Fuqua released a joint statement: "We cannot in good conscience provide economic support to a government that enacts regressive voting laws that are designed to restrict voter access".
